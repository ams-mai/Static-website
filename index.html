I was born on the month of October, a scorpio. Most people at present references a zodiac sign for everything but I just recently learned the obsession behind it and I personally think signs like that does not define a person's quality and characteristics (except if you're a capricorn).

I am the eldest daughter in our family. I have a younger sister that completes our family of four. Some may think that we're a small family, but for me, we are just the exact size. In my father's side of the family, I claimed the throne of being the eldest grandchild. It definitely felt good at first but I guess everything do come to an end.

Since the beginning of my life, I gazed upon Puerto Galera. We may have moved and lived at three different barangays but nothing compares to a place we built called home. Puerto Galera is place full of beaches and mountains. It is quiet and peaceful--perfect for vacations and rests. Tourists often visit my hometown but I rarely encounter one because the barangay I live in is far from attracting spots.

During my childhood, my parents were busy at work. I learned to become independent and entertained myself since I am 9. It wasn't that bad in fact it made me learn lots of skills. However, because of those moments in my life, I am raised to be timid. I often become awkward with peers and voluntarily choose to be alone most times.

Regrets

I regret loads of things.

My life has been a series of trial and error. Everything I do just reflects how indecisive and inconsistent I am.

I regret my childhood. I regret being a child achiever because it made me thirst for appreciation and recognition. I regret being independent because it made fear relying on others and spiked my obsession to control. I regret growing up so fast that made me enjoy less of those playful times.

I regret my high school life. I regret not being energetic and appealing which made me be consumed by academic anxiety and pressure. I regret wasting my junior years with a guy that traumatised and blinded my youthful mind. I regret not going out more often with friends and classmates which made me incapable of socializing to peers. I regret holing myself in my comfortzone which made me just the same as before.

I regret my senior high years. I regret being the pressuring classmate that I hated before. I regret not involving much of my classmates as response to my obsession to control. I regret hating myself for being incompetent and unable to achieve as much as before.

There are situations in our lives that give us opportunities to choose while there are situations that we are just left in between.

I didn't live a wonderful life, that's for sure, but I can say that I have made my younger self proud for surviving and continuing despite those regrets.
